INEFFECTIVE STUDY HABITS AND SOLUTIONS

Ineffective study habits is a barrier for many students and adults, it can be whether they have distractions, procrastinations, cramming, or the lack of amount of sleep they get etc. This website will allow students and adults to figure out what ineffective study habits they have and what solutions will allow them to fix their ineffective habits and turn them into effective study habits.

Studying without a plan

When studying without a plan there will be a lack of direction which will cause students and adults to find it difficult to cover all their subjects and work within the limited amount of time they have.A solution for this is to start setting up a routine, timtetable, or schedule, this way your able to see when you're free to study that way your studies and your extra curricular or family events dont interfere with each other and you will be able to get more work done during a time with no distractions and you will not need to worry about anything else till the time your study ends.

Cramming

Many people decide to leave their work til last minute, which can affect them badly, causing them to study everything before the due date. A solution for this is to study some of your work early and then study some more when you have time, this way you wont need to cram every single work together.

Distractions

It is very common for people to get distracted whenever it comes to studying due to social media, family, and friends, this habit can affect people to delay their studies and become behind in classes. A solution that will help poeple study without becoming distracted is to either place their phone in a different room where it is nowhere to be seen or you could either study in a quiet area such as the library, that way there are no distractions and if family is one of the distractions then it is recommended to study at a library where there is silence and no distractions, allowing you to focus and study everything you need to catch up on or any work.

Studying when you're tired

Studing without having enough sleep is inefficient, you will not be able to focus as much and remember notes while studying. Therefore, it is important that you ensure that you have had enough sleep and rest, that way when you have enough energy you will be able to stay productive when studying. Another important thing to look out for is when you have had enough sleep and you are very energized then you can try studying a bit during that time.

Multitasking

Multitasking is a common thing for many people, they tend to focus on more than one subject during the limited amount of time they have. A solution for this is to divide your work and pay attention to the subject that you find more difficult and then shift your attention to other subjects once you have finished and it is also recommended to leave the subject you find the least difficult for last, almost like a treat.

Not asking for help when needed

Many people are afraid to ask help from teachers or peers when in need of assistance. Without asking for assistance this will not help when it comes to studying due to the lack of knowledge you have. It is important that you correct this bad habit as it wont only help improve your grades but will also allow you to overcome that fear and improve your skills. By starting off small you can ask for help from your friends and classmates and then once you feel more confident in asking, you can start asking for help from your teachers or tutors. Remember it is a teachers job to help you.

Having notes everywhere

This is a main bad habit that people have, having notes placed everywhere will not be helpful as it will be difficult for you to find notes for a certain subject as it is piled up with every other subject notes. Additionally, this will cause some people to panick as they may have only one copy and have not memorised what was on it or some may have to rewrite their notes which causes them to delay their studies. Therefore, it is important to prioritise having a neat work space where it is easy for you to find notes for a specific subject.
